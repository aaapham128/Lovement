Introduction

#LoveWins represents the ongoing LGBTQ+ movement advocating for the rights and equality of those who identify as gay, lesbian, bisexual, transgender, queer, questioning, intersex, and nonbinary. On June 28, 1969, the police officers of New York City raided a gay bar called the Stonewall and it caused a riot that pushed the modern civil movement of the modern gay rights activism. In 1993, President Clinton passed the Don’t Ask, Don’t Tell policy, which banned openly gay male and female Americans from the army. The Don’t Ask Don’t Tell policy was repealed in September of 2011. The United States Supreme Court, the highest court in the United States court system, ruled same sex marriages are consitutional in all 50 states and the states can’t prohibit same sex couples from marrying.

Some of the issues of the LGBTQ+ community are facing currently are:
  • Hate crimes and discrimmination against LGBTQ+ people
  • LGBTQ+ couples are turned away from adopting children in foster care
    • Transgender students are not allowed to use the restroom
